Θεοδώρα ΣιζοπούλουTheodora Sizopoulou

Η Θεοδώρα Σιζοπούλου είναι Ψυχολόγος, απόφοιτη του Τμήματος Ψυχολογίας του Αριστοτελείου Πανεπιστημίου Θεσσαλονίκης, με μεταπτυχιακό τίτλο στην Κλινική και Κοινωνική Ψυχολογία των Εξαρτήσεων από το ίδιο Πανεπιστήμιο. Έχει εκπαιδευτεί και πιστοποιηθεί στη Συστημική – Οικογενειακή Θεραπεία και στη θεραπεία του ψυχικού τραύματος. Είναι ευρωπαϊκά πιστοποιημένη θεραπεύτρια EMDR (EMDR Europe Accredited Practitioner) και επόπτρια (EMDR Europe Consultant in training) σύμφωνα με τα κριτήρια της EMDR Europe. Επίσης διαθέτει επιπλέον εξειδίκευση στη δυναμική ομάδων, στη διαχείριση πένθους και στη θεραπεία των εξαρτήσεων. Παρακολουθεί τις επιστημονικές εξελίξεις στο χώρο της Ψυχολογίας και συμμετέχει σε επιστημονικά συνέδρια με ανακοινώσεις όπως και σε ερευνητικά προγράμματα. Δεν σταματά να εκπαιδεύεται με στόχο την καλύτερη ποιότητα υπηρεσιών σε όσους την εμπιστεύονται.

Εργάστηκε για 24 χρόνια στο ΚΕΘΕΑ ΙΘΑΚΗ, 22 από τα οποία σε θέσεις ευθύνης, όπως στο Συμβουλευτικό Κέντρο, το Κέντρο Οικογενειακής Υποστήριξης, τη Μονάδα Διπλής Διάγνωσης και τη Θεραπευτική Κοινότητα. Παράλληλα, διετέλεσε επόπτρια πρακτικής άσκησης φοιτητών Ψυχολογίας του Αριστοτελείου Πανεπιστημίου από το 2002, συμβάλλοντας ενεργά στη διαμόρφωση νέων επαγγελματιών ψυχικής υγείας.

Με πολυετή εμπειρία στον χώρο της ψυχοθεραπείας και της απεξάρτησης, καθώς και βαθιά ανθρωποκεντρική προσέγγιση, η κ. Σιζοπούλου προσφέρει ένα ασφαλές και υποστηρικτικό θεραπευτικό πλαίσιο για άτομα, ζεύγη και οικογένειες, εστιάζοντας στην ενδυνάμωση, την ψυχική ανθεκτικότητα και την αποκατάσταση της εσωτερικής ισορροπίας και ευημερίας.

A deeply human-centered approach, grounded in many years of psychotherapy and addiction recovery practice.

Θεοδώρα ΣιζοπούλουTheodora Sizopoulou

Theodora Sizopoulou is a Psychologist, a graduate of the Department of Psychology at the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, holding a Master's degree in the Clinical and Social Psychology of Addictions from the same University. She is trained and certified in Systemic – Family Therapy and in the treatment of psychological trauma. She is a European-accredited EMDR therapist (EMDR Europe Accredited Practitioner) and a consultant (EMDR Europe Consultant in training) in line with EMDR Europe criteria. She also holds further specialization in group dynamics, grief management and addiction treatment. She follows scientific developments in the field of Psychology and takes part in scientific conferences with presentations, as well as in research programs. She never stops training, aiming for the highest quality of care for those who trust her.

She worked for 24 years at KETHEA ITHAKI, 22 of them in positions of responsibility — at the Counseling Center, the Family Support Center, the Dual Diagnosis Unit and the Therapeutic Community. In parallel, she has served as a clinical-practice supervisor for Psychology students of the Aristotle University since 2002, actively contributing to the development of new mental-health professionals.

With years of experience in psychotherapy and addiction recovery, and a deeply human-centered approach, Ms. Sizopoulou offers a safe and supportive therapeutic space for individuals, couples and families — focusing on empowerment, psychological resilience and the restoration of inner balance and well-being.

Τι είναι η Θεραπεία EMDR;

What is EMDR Therapy?

Μία σύγχρονη προσέγγιση στην αντιμετώπιση τραύματος και στρες

A modern approach to treating trauma and stress

Ολοκληρωμένη Περιγραφή της Θεραπείας EMDR

Η θεραπεία EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing – Απευαισθητοποίηση και Επανεπεξεργασία μέσω αμφίπλευρου διεστιακού ερεθισμού) αποτελεί μια πρωτοποριακή, συνθετική και επιστημονικά τεκμηριωμένη ψυχοθεραπευτική προσέγγιση για την αντιμετώπιση τραυματικών βιωμάτων, φοβιών, άγχους και άλλων ψυχολογικών δυσκολιών. Στοχεύει στην ανακούφιση της δυσφορίας που σχετίζεται με παρελθοντικά τραυματικά ή στρεσογόνα βιώματα, στην αντιμετώπιση των συμπτωμάτων που είναι αποτέλεσμα αντίξοων εμπειριών αλλά και στις ανησυχίες για το μέλλον.

Πώς λειτουργεί η θεραπεία EMDR;

Η θεραπεία EMDR βασίζεται στη φυσική ικανότητα του εγκεφάλου να επεξεργάζεται και να αφομοιώνει δύσκολες ή τραυματικές εμπειρίες. Πραγματοποιείται με τη συνεργασία θεραπευτή και θεραπευόμενου, σε ένα ασφαλές και υποστηρικτικό περιβάλλον. Ο θεραπευτής καθοδηγεί το άτομο στην αναγνώριση και επεξεργασία τραυματικών αναμνήσεων, χρησιμοποιώντας αμφίπλευρη διέγερση (συνήθως κινήσεις των ματιών).

A Complete Overview of EMDR Therapy

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is a pioneering, integrative and evidence-based psychotherapeutic approach for addressing traumatic experiences, phobias, anxiety and other psychological difficulties. It aims to relieve the distress linked to past traumatic or stressful experiences, to treat symptoms resulting from adverse experiences, and to ease worries about the future.

How does EMDR work?

EMDR draws on the brain's natural ability to process and integrate difficult or traumatic experiences. It takes place in collaboration between therapist and client, within a safe and supportive environment. The therapist guides the person in recognizing and processing traumatic memories using bilateral stimulation (usually eye movements).

Through guided eye movements (or other bilateral stimuli such as sounds or tactile signals), the client recalls traumatic or distressing memories while the process of reprocessing and discharging negative emotions, self-beliefs and physical discomfort is activated. This helps the person form and integrate new, more positive beliefs and more adaptive coping mechanisms. Structured therapeutic protocols are applied to enhance effectiveness.

Who is EMDR for?

EMDR is intended for people who have experienced traumatic events (such as accidents, loss, abuse), as well as those facing chronic anxiety, panic attacks, phobias, depression or difficulties in their relationships. EMDR is applied both to adults and to children and adolescents.

Benefits of EMDR therapy

  • Reduction of trauma and anxiety symptoms
  • Improved self-esteem and psychological resilience
  • Relief from past distressing experiences that affect the present
  • Strengthened ability to manage stressful situations

Why choose EMDR therapy?

EMDR is recommended by international mental-health organizations (such as the World Health Organization) for treating trauma and post-traumatic stress. Its effectiveness has been demonstrated through numerous scientific studies. If you are looking for a safe, proven and non-invasive method to address difficult emotions and experiences, EMDR can offer you the tools for meaningful change and relief.

What is addiction treatment

Addiction treatment is a multidimensional process aimed at ending the use of addictive substances or behaviors, as well as restoring the person's physical, mental and social health. The modern approach recognizes that addiction is not only a medical problem, but is often linked to psychological, social and environmental factors, requiring a holistic response.

The psychosocial approach at the core of recovery

The psychosocial approach focuses on addressing the psychological and social factors associated with addiction. Its primary goal is to help the person understand the deeper causes of addiction, strengthen coping skills and build up self-esteem and a sense of control over their life.

Key elements of the psychosocial approach

  • Group therapy: Provides a safe environment for sharing experiences, mutual support and developing communication and cooperation skills. It strengthens a sense of belonging and reduces social stigma.
  • Individual psychotherapy: Aims to explore the personal causes of addiction, strengthen defense mechanisms and develop new strategies for managing stress and emotions.
  • Family counseling: Strengthens the family's involvement and understanding, shaping a supportive environment for the person.
  • Education and reintegration: Helps in acquiring new knowledge and skills that facilitate social and professional reintegration.

Long-term maintenance of abstinence

The psychosocial approach focuses not only on ending use, but also on preventing relapse. It strengthens resilience and the management of stress and challenges, while promoting autonomy and quality of life. By creating a supportive network, the person gains the tools to face difficulties without returning to addiction.

What is Family Therapy?

Family therapy is a specialized form of psychotherapy addressed to the whole family, or to members of it facing difficulties in relationships, communication, or serious events affecting the dynamics of the family system. The goal of therapy is to strengthen cohesion, understanding and support among members, creating an environment of acceptance and safety.

Who is it for?

Family therapy is indicated for families dealing with:

  • Frequent conflicts, tensions or misunderstandings
  • Communication difficulties or patterns of estrangement
  • Transitional periods (divorce, loss, relocation, new members)
  • Behavioral issues in children or adolescents
  • Managing the mental or physical illness of a member
  • Addictions or other serious difficulties

What are the benefits of family therapy?

  • Improved communication and emotional understanding
  • Development of functional ways to resolve conflicts
  • Strengthened trust and mutual support
  • Empowerment of individual and collective coping skills
  • Cultivation of a positive climate and cohesion within the family

How does family therapy work?

Therapy takes place in group sessions with the whole family or with individual members, depending on needs. The therapist acts as a neutral mediator, helping participants freely express their feelings and thoughts, recognize the behavior patterns that hinder harmony, and develop new, more functional ways of communicating and cooperating.

Why choose family therapy?
